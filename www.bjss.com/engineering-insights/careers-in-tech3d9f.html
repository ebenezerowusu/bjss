What I wish I knew before starting a career in tech: pearls from the BJSS webinar

Written by Adam Roche | Aug 16, 2021 1:41:35 PM

In our recent webinar, three BJSS software engineers joined Dan Webb, head of the BJSS Academy, to talk about their experiences of getting into tech. They also shared tips on searching for a great place to work and dealing with interviews. 

First up, Ruth Berry explained that BJSS is a consultancy that works for many clients in different sectors. These clients rely on software but don’t always know too much about software development and testing. The code that software engineers like Ruth write must be really clear because the client might want to support it themselves.

As well as the diversity of clients, BJSS software engineers get to work with many different languages. In her two years with BJSS, Ruth has already worked on three different projects in three different languages. Working for an in-house software organisation rather than a consultancy like BJSS might mean focusing on a smaller number of problems and maybe a specific language.

Wellbeing and experience

Ruth highlighted wellbeing as a key concern to think about when looking for a company to join. Being cared for is just as important as getting the job done. She explained that everyone at BJSS is supported by a line manager and a squad leader. BJSS also offers access to therapy if you need it and support sessions at lunchtime including rock climbing, yoga and football, all paid for by the company. Ruth said that BJSS is famous for its socials, including weekend summer festivals where employees, partners and families can get together and kick back.

Ruth recommended that people thinking of going into tech show some prior experience – whether that’s coding up your own simple game or building a website around something that interests you. There are many free learning materials online that you can use, and YouTube is great for ideas and how-tos.
