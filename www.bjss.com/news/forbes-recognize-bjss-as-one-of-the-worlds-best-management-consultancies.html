    Forbes recognises BJSS as one of the World's Best Management Consulting Firms in 2022

    16 Aug, 2022

    In collaboration with Statista, Forbes has identified BJSS as one of the world's leading consulting companies in its inaugural World's Best Management Consulting Firms report. The list recognises the world's top 230 most recommended management consulting firms. BJSS has ranked as one of the leading consultancies in Healthcare, Energy, Commodities & Utilities, Consumer Goods & Retail, and I.T. Strategy & Implementation.

    "On behalf of the team here at BJSS, we want to thank our clients and partners for recommending us as one of the world's leading management consulting firms," says Hannah Tharp, Head of Consulting at BJSS. "Our clients face one of the most uncertain times in their histories, and we're committed to helping them overcome their challenges to deliver positive business outcomes. We want to take this moment to celebrate our inclusion in the Forbes report. Still, we remain focused on expanding our team and evolving our capabilities to help our clients thrive during this difficult time."

    BJSS' inclusion in the Forbes World's Best Management Consulting Firms report follows the company's recognition as a Financial Times U.K. Leading Management Consultant for the last three years.

    Please click here to view the complete Forbes World's Best Management Consulting Firms list.

    Research Methodology

    Statista partnered with Forbes to compile the World's Best Management Consulting Firms report. Statista established several worldwide top lists in the field of management consultancies, and has conducted such analyses across the U.S., Germany, France, the U.K., Switzerland and Japan. To determine this list, Statista looked at the results of these country-wide top lists and created a top list of the World's Best Management Consulting Firms. All six top lists are based on a similar methodology and take into account numerous recommendations from consultants and clients of consulting firms. The ranking is divided into 13 sectors—from aerospace and defence to financial institutions—and 14 functional areas—including strategy, sustainability and digital transformation. The 230 firms that received the most recommendations are ranked according to star ratings: five stars for "very frequently recommended", four stars for "frequently recommended" and three stars for "recommended."
